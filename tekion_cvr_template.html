The Customer Value Review is a structured, outcome-focused conversation between Tekion and dealer leadership. It replaces the traditional QBR format with a meeting designed around what the dealer is trying to achieve — not what Tekion wants to show. The CVM facilitates. The CVA contributes data. The dealer leaves with documented progress, visible gaps, and a clear next chapter.

Who's in the Room — Maturity-Calibrated Participation

The CVR is not the same meeting for every dealer. Participant mix, conversation depth, and format adapt based on where the dealer sits in the Dealer Maturity Model.

Meeting Structure — Five-Block Agenda

01 Dealer Goals & Context Reset CVM 10 min ►

Open with the dealer's world — not Tekion's. This block reanchors the conversation to what the dealer said mattered in the Customer Value Plan and surfaces anything that has changed.

Confirm or update stated dealer priorities from the CVP — what are they trying to achieve this quarter?
Acknowledge any operational changes since the last touchpoint — staffing, volume, market shifts.
Surface anything from the Check-In history that warrants deeper discussion today.
Confirm the success criteria the dealer will use to measure this period — in their words, not Tekion's.
02 Platform Performance & Adoption Narrative CVA-led 20 min ►

The CVA presents adoption data not as a scorecard — but as a story about where the dealer is gaining traction, where gaps remain, and what those gaps cost them. Data leads to action, not defense.

Module utilization by department — what is being used fully, partially, or not at all.
Maturity tier assessment against benchmarks — where does this dealer rank within their cohort?
Named workflows with high impact that remain underutilized — the "leaving value on the table" conversation.
User engagement signals — power users, adoption laggards, training needs.
CVA prep requirement This block requires CVA preparation before the meeting: pull current adoption data, identify 2–3 specific high-value gaps, and build the narrative connecting those gaps to the dealer's stated goals from Block 01. Do not present raw numbers — present a story.
03 Value Realized — Progress Against Goals CVM + CVA 20 min ►

Connect platform data to business outcomes in language the dealer owns. This is the core of why the CVR replaces the QBR — it demonstrates value, not activity. The CVM drives the conversation; the CVA supports with evidence.

Review documented success criteria from the CVP — what was the dealer trying to achieve, and what is the evidence of movement?
Where value is clear, make it explicit and documented — this becomes the renewal evidence base.
Where value is unclear or lagging, name the gap honestly — then connect it to a specific action.
Dealer maturity progression — has the account moved within the Dealer Maturity Model this period?
Phase 1 note In early deployment, documented value evidence may be limited. If the dealer is pre-maturity-baseline, use this block to establish the starting point — the benchmark against which next quarter's CVR will measure progress. A defined starting line is itself a valuable output.
04 Next Quarter — Joint Priorities & Commitments CVM 15 min ►

The CVR must produce tangible next steps — not a list of follow-ups that disappear into email. This block turns the conversation into a commitment from both sides. The dealer owns some of it. Tekion owns the rest.

2–3 specific Tekion commitments for the next quarter — what will the CVM and CVA do, and by when?
1–2 dealer-side actions — adoption behaviors, workflow activations, internal change management steps they agreed to own.
Next maturity milestone — what does "progress" look like in 90 days? Define it explicitly so the next CVR has a clear measuring stick.
Confirm any CVA engagement needed between now and next CVR — training, adoption sprints, diagnostic follow-ups.
05 Relationship & Forward Look CVM 10 min ►

Close the CVR with the dealer feeling heard and the CVM with a clear read on relationship health. This is also the natural entry point for expansion conversations at Tier 3 accounts where the relationship is strong enough to support it.

Open question: What would make the next 90 days a clear win for you and your team?
Any concerns or risks the dealer wants to name — surface them before they escalate.
For Tier 3 accounts: natural bridge to expansion or growth conversation if relationship and value evidence support it.
CVM confirms CVR summary will be shared within 48 hours — dealer has a record, not just memories.

What This Means for Managers

The CVR creates new accountability infrastructure for the CS leadership team. Managers will need to:

→ Inspect CVR readiness before each meeting ►
Review the CVM's CVP before the CVR is delivered. Is the dealer's goal context current? Does the CVA have adoption data ready? Has the prior quarter's commitment progress been logged? The CVP is the pre-meeting brief — not an optional document.
→ Review CVR summaries as a portfolio health signal ►
The 48-hour CVR summary becomes a management data point — not a dealer communication. Patterns across summaries will surface which accounts are advancing, which are stalling, and where CVM or CVA coaching is needed. This replaces anecdotal pipeline conversations with documented evidence.
→ Attend selectively — with purpose, not as oversight ►
Manager presence in a CVR should be a signal — not a default. Attend Tier 3 strategic accounts to reinforce executive relationship. Attend high-risk accounts to demonstrate commitment. Avoid attending routine Tier 1 CVRs unless the CVM needs development support — it dilutes the CVM's ownership and the dealer's experience.
Phase 1 context for this artifact
This framework introduces the Customer Value Review concept to leadership and defines the standard. The working CVM template — with structured pre-meeting checklist, CVA data brief, and post-meeting summary format — will be developed in Phase 2 alongside CVM enablement. The goal here is alignment on what the CVR is, why it replaces the QBR, and what leadership will need to do to make it operational.
