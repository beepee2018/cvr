Customer Value Review
The Customer Value Review is a structured, outcome-focused conversation between Tekion and dealer leadership. It replaces the traditional QBR format with a meeting designed around what the dealer is trying to achieve — not what Tekion wants to show. The CVM facilitates. The CVA contributes data. The dealer leaves with documented progress, visible gaps, and a clear next chapter.

Logistics and Participants

Who's in the Room — Maturity-Calibrated Participation

The CVR is not the same meeting for every dealer. Participant mix, conversation depth, and format adapt based on where the dealer sits in the Dealer Maturity Model. The DMM tiers are: Operating → Accelerating → Optimizing → Leading.

Maturity tier Dealer participants CVR focus Format
Tier 1 — Operating GM or Ops lead; department heads as needed Foundation check — are core workflows stable and in consistent use? Establish the baseline for future measurement. Focus on Accounting, Parts, Service, and Retail. 60 min; CVM-led with CVA data summary; listening posture
Tier 2 — Accelerating GM and 1–2 department leads Differentiated feature adoption — what Tekion-specific capabilities are in use, and where are the gaps? First formal value evidence and emerging outcomes. 60–75 min; CVM-led, CVA presents adoption narrative
Tier 3 — Optimizing GM and department leads; cross-functional where expansion modules span teams Full platform performance vs. benchmarks; expansion module activation (CRM, TekionPay, Analytics); documented value realized; next maturity milestone. 75–90 min; CVM-led, CVA presents full adoption brief
Tier 4 — Leading Dealer principal and/or GM; ownership group when relevant AI-driven insights and platform as competitive advantage; documented ROI; co-investment and expansion conversation; roadmap visibility; reference and co-marketing potential. 90 min; executive-to-executive; semi-annual strategic conversation — not account management

Meeting Structure — Five-Block Agenda

01 Dealer Goals & Context Reset CVM 10 min ►

Open with the dealer's world — not Tekion's. This block reanchors the conversation to what the dealer said mattered in the Customer Value Plan and surfaces anything that has changed. You are not presenting yet — you are confirming.

Confirm or update stated dealer priorities from the CVP — what are they trying to achieve this quarter?
Acknowledge any operational changes since the last touchpoint — staffing, volume, market shifts, new ownership dynamics.
If open support tickets exist, name them now — briefly, with ownership and timeline. Five minutes maximum. This earns the right to the value conversation.
Confirm the success criteria the dealer will use to measure this period — in their words, not Tekion's.
Tier-calibrated opening
OperatingKeep the opening light — this dealer is still building confidence in the platform. Lead with curiosity: "What's been working well, and where is the team still finding friction?"
AcceleratingReference specific CVP goals from last quarter. Ask what has changed in the business that might shift their priorities for the next 90 days.
OptimizingReference prior CVR commitments directly. Open with a brief scorecard: "Here's what we committed to last quarter — here's what happened."
LeadingOpen at the ownership/strategic level — reference competitive context, market position, or group-level performance before getting into platform specifics.
02 Platform Performance & Adoption Narrative CVA-led 20 min ►

The CVA presents adoption data not as a scorecard — but as a story about where the dealer is gaining traction, where gaps remain, and what those gaps cost them. Data leads to action, not defense.

Module utilization by department — foundation modules (Accounting, Parts, Service, Retail) and any active expansion modules (CRM, TekionPay, Analytics, AI).
Maturity tier assessment — where does this dealer sit within Operating / Accelerating / Optimizing / Leading, and what is the evidence for that placement?
Named workflows with high impact that remain underutilized — the "leaving value on the table" conversation.
User engagement signals — power users, adoption laggards, departments with visible progress versus those stalling.
CVA prep requirement This block requires CVA preparation before the meeting: pull current adoption data, confirm the dealer's current DMM tier, identify 2–3 specific high-value gaps, and build the narrative connecting those gaps to the dealer's stated goals from Block 01. Do not present raw numbers — present a story. The data brief should be shared with the CVM before the meeting, not assembled during it.
03 Value Realized — Progress Against Goals CVM + CVA 20 min ►

Connect platform data to business outcomes in language the dealer owns. This is the core of why the CVR replaces the QBR — it demonstrates value, not activity. The CVM drives the conversation; the CVA supports with evidence.

Review documented success criteria from the CVP — what was the dealer trying to achieve, and what is the evidence of movement?
Where value is clear, make it explicit and documented — this becomes the renewal evidence base.
Where value is unclear or lagging, name the gap honestly — then connect it to a specific action, not a vague commitment.
Maturity progression — has this dealer moved within the DMM this period? If not, what is the specific barrier?
Value framing by tier
OperatingValue evidence may be limited. If no baseline exists yet, use this block to establish it — the starting point from which future CVRs will measure progress. A defined starting line is itself a valuable output.
AcceleratingConnect differentiated feature usage to emerging results. Even early evidence matters — name it, document it, anchor it to something the dealer said they care about.
OptimizingTie expansion module activation to measurable operational impact. Compare against prior quarter benchmarks. Reference what the dealer committed to last CVR and show the delta.
LeadingArticulate compounding value — how does each layer of the platform build on results already achieved? This dealer can tell the story themselves. Let them.
04 Next Quarter — Joint Priorities & Commitments CVM 15 min ►

The CVR must produce tangible next steps — not a list of follow-ups that disappear into email. This block turns the conversation into a commitment from both sides. The dealer owns some of it. Tekion owns the rest. Both sides leave knowing what was agreed.

2–3 specific Tekion commitments for the next quarter — what will the CVM and CVA do, and by when? Named, dated, owned.
1–2 dealer-side actions — adoption behaviors, workflow activations, internal change management steps they agreed to own.
Next maturity milestone — what does progress look like in 90 days? Define it explicitly in terms of DMM tier movement or expansion module activation so the next CVR has a clear measuring stick.
Confirm any CVA engagement needed between now and next CVR — training sessions, adoption sprints, diagnostic follow-ups, or platform walkthroughs.
CVP update trigger Everything committed in this block must be logged in the CVP before the dealer summary is sent. Commitments that live only in meeting notes are not commitments — they are intentions. The CVP is the record.
05 Relationship & Forward Look CVM 10 min ►

Close the CVR with the dealer feeling heard and the CVM with a clear read on relationship health. At Tier 4 / Leading accounts, this is also the natural entry point for expansion, co-marketing, or executive relationship conversations.

Open question: What would make the next 90 days a clear win for you and your team?
Any concerns or risks the dealer wants to name — surface them before they escalate.
For Tier 4 / Leading accounts: natural bridge to expansion or growth conversation if relationship and value evidence support it.
CVM confirms CVR summary will be shared within 48 hours — dealer has a record, not just memories.

When Support Issues Are in the Room

Unresolved support tickets don't cancel the CVR — but ignoring them will. Dealers who are sitting on open issues will spend the entire value conversation thinking about them. The CVM's job is to acknowledge the support reality first, briefly and with ownership, before the value conversation begins. This clears the air and earns the right to move forward.
What not to do
Walk in and proceed as if open tickets don't exist — dealers notice the avoidance immediately.
Spend CVR time on support triage — that's a different conversation with a different owner.
Make promises in the CVR that require support resolution to deliver — this creates compounding credibility risk.
Let a stack of tickets reframe the entire account narrative — one bad period is not the full picture.
What the CVM does instead
Open Block 01 with a brief, direct acknowledgment — name the open items, state the owner, state the timeline. Five minutes maximum.
Separate support accountability from value accountability — the CVM owns the relationship and escalation path, not the resolution itself.
Use the CVP to show context — if a dealer is behind on adoption due to an unresolved issue, name that explicitly. Data without context is unfair.
Document open items in the CVR summary with a committed resolution date — accountability without derailing the meeting.
When to pause the CVR — escalation threshold
⚠ A routine CVR proceeds when tickets are open but not blocking core operations. Acknowledge, assign, move on. The value conversation is still valid and necessary.
✗ Shift to a recovery conversation when a dealer has a broken core workflow, is in active escalation, or has explicitly said they are not ready for a business review. Reschedule once stability is restored — and document the decision in the CVP.
→ Manager call: The threshold between "proceed with acknowledgment" and "pause and recover" is a judgment call that managers and CVMs should calibrate together in 1:1 coaching. It is not left to individual CVM discretion in the field.

What This Means for Managers

The CVR creates new accountability infrastructure for the CS leadership team. Managers will need to:

→ Inspect CVR readiness before each meeting ►
Review the CVM's CVP before the CVR is delivered. Is the dealer's goal context current? Is the DMM tier confirmed? Does the CVA have the adoption brief ready? Have prior quarter commitments been logged? The CVP is the pre-meeting brief — not an optional document. A CVR that walks in without a current CVP is a CVR that isn't ready to run.
→ Review CVR summaries as a portfolio health signal ►
The 48-hour CVR summary becomes a management data point — not just a dealer communication. Patterns across summaries will surface which accounts are advancing toward higher DMM tiers, which are stalling, and where CVM or CVA coaching is needed. This replaces anecdotal pipeline conversations with documented evidence and gives managers a structured view of the portfolio that didn't exist before.
→ Attend selectively — with purpose, not as oversight ►
Manager presence in a CVR should be a signal — not a default. Attend Tier 4 / Leading accounts to reinforce the executive relationship and model what a strategic conversation looks like. Attend high-risk accounts to demonstrate commitment. Avoid attending routine Tier 1 / Operating CVRs unless the CVM needs development support — it dilutes the CVM's ownership and the dealer's experience of being in a peer-level conversation.
→ Use DMM tier movement as a performance signal ►
Accounts that remain in the same DMM tier across two consecutive CVR cycles warrant a coaching conversation — not a performance flag, but a problem-solving one. Is the barrier adoption behavior, a platform gap, a staffing issue at the dealer, or a CVM conversation quality issue? CVR summaries make this visible. Use them to coach forward, not backward.
